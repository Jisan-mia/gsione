## **Biography**

Asheer Shah is the Founder and Director of the Governance and Security Initiative (GSi), an independent policy research platform dedicated to advancing governance solutions for cybersecurity, emerging technologies, climate security, and international affairs. His work focuses on the intersection of digital security, artificial intelligence governance, and geopolitical risk in an increasingly technology-driven world.

Asheer holds a Master’s degree in Cybersecurity Governance from Leiden University in the Netherlands, where his research focused on cyber espionage, digital crime, behavioral cybersecurity, and artificial intelligence governance. He previously completed a Bachelor’s degree in Global Studies and Governance with a minor in Economics from Independent University, Bangladesh.

He has worked with leading research and policy institutions, including the Clingendael Institute in the Netherlands, where he delivered training programs on global AI governance models for diplomats and humanitarian professionals. He also served as a Research Associate at the Bangladesh Institute of Peace and Security Studies, contributing to policy research on cyber governance, misinformation, climate security, and regional strategic affairs.

Asheer’s research spans cybersecurity policy, artificial intelligence governance, digital security, and the geopolitical implications of emerging technologies. His work has appeared in academic publications, policy platforms, and international media outlets. Through GSi, he leads interdisciplinary research projects and policy dialogues aimed at strengthening governance frameworks for emerging technologies and global security challenges.
